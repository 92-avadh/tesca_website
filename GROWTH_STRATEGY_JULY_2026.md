## Executive Summary

### The business as the website presents it
- **Brand:** TESCA Visa Consultancy, operating since **2005** (~20 years), two branches in **Surat, Gujarat** (Simada Naka + Mota Varachha).
- **Positioning:** Premium study-abroad & student-visa consultancy. Hero line: *"Don't Just Study Abroad. Build a Bigger Future."*
- **Headline trust claims:** 97% visa success rate, 30,000+ students placed, since 2005.
- **Tech:** Astro (SSR) on Cloudflare, Supabase backend, GA4 installed. Site is fast, modern, and visually premium — genuinely above the category average in design quality.
- **Primary conversion paths today:** (1) *Book a Counsellor* modal (`openCounsellorForm`), (2) *Check Eligibility* 5-step university-matcher. Both feed leads to email/Supabase.

### The 5 highest-leverage problems (ranked)
1. **No WhatsApp capture.** In India, WhatsApp is the #1 lead channel for this category. Your site has **no floating WhatsApp button**; WhatsApp appears *only* as a fallback after a form *fails*. This alone is likely costing you 25–40% of capturable intent. **(Fix in Week 1.)**
2. **No Meta Pixel / no conversion events.** You have GA4 but no Meta Pixel and no `gtag` conversion events firing on form submit. You literally **cannot run optimised Meta or Google lead-gen ads** without this. **(Fix Day 1–2 — it is a hard dependency for Part 6.)**
3. **Service–intent mismatch** (the box above). The site can't capture the non-student visa demand the brief targets.
4. **Thin SEO surface for high-intent queries.** Countries live as anchor sections on *one* page (`/countries#ca`), not indexable country pages. No blog. No refusal/PR/work-permit/visitor content. You are invisible for the exact commercial keywords that convert.
5. **Trust signals are claimed, not proven.** "97%/99%" appears inconsistently (97% in hero, 99% in the form and meta description). No Google Reviews widget, no star aggregate, no counsellor credentials/photos, no third-party badges (ICEF/British Council/QEAC). Claims without proof *reduce* trust with savvy buyers.

### The plan to 100+ qualified leads in July (realistic model)

### Detailed findings by audit dimension

**Trust issues**
- **Inconsistent success rate:** Hero = **97%**, soft-modal = **97%**, but the counsellor form sidebar = **99%** and the home `<meta description>` = **"99% visa success rate."** Pick one number you can defend in writing and use it *everywhere*. Inconsistency is the #1 credibility tell.
- **Unproven claims:** "30,000+ students," "97%," "Since 2005" appear as text only. No Google rating, no review count, no verifiable proof, no logos of universities/partners, no counsellor names/photos/credentials. The *Voices from Community* testimonials are hardcoded (Vivek Godhani, Vidhi Gujrati, etc.) with no photo, no destination, no year — they read as placeholder.
- **No third-party authority badges:** No ICEF, British Council (IELTS), QEAC, university-partner, or MARA/ICCRC-equivalent marks. For visa buyers, third-party validation outperforms self-claims.
- **Footer "Security Audit" + dev-portfolio link** signal "developer's pet project," not "trusted 20-year institution."

**UX / visual hierarchy**
- Design quality is genuinely strong (premium typography, gradients, motion via Lenis/Framer/GSAP). This is an asset — most competitors look dated by comparison.
- **Two FAQ blocks on the homepage** (an inline 5-item accordion *and* the `<FAQ>` component with 8 items). Redundant, lengthens the page, and splits schema. Consolidate to one schema-backed FAQ.
- **Heavy client JS** (React islands: AICounsellor, CounsellorForm, EligibilityForm, carousels, calculators) — watch mobile LCP/INP. Hero already has `fetchpriority=high` (good, per prior audit).

**CTA problems**
- Primary CTA is good and consistent ("Speak to Our Counsellor" / "Book Free Counselling" → modal). But:
  - **No sticky mobile CTA bar** (Call + WhatsApp + Book) — the single biggest mobile-conversion miss.
  - **No WhatsApp CTA anywhere** except post-failure.
  - The eligibility result page's final CTA ("Speak to Our Counsellor") is strong — replicate that pattern site-wide.

**Mobile experience**
- Responsive and clean (hamburger drawer, mobile CTA in drawer). But the *drawer* hides Call/WhatsApp behind a tap — they should be **always-visible sticky** on mobile.
- Hero is `min-h-[65vh]` with 4-image slider every 4.5s — fine, but verify the slider images aren't hurting LCP on 4G (Surat audience is heavily mobile + variable network).

**Navigation**
- Mega-menus for Destinations/Services are excellent. Gaps: **no Contact, no About, no Visitor-Visa/PR/Work-Permit entries.** Add a top-level "Visas" menu when those pages exist.

**Messaging**
- Tightly focused on students — *good for that audience, invisible for everyone else.* A parent searching "USA visitor visa from Surat" finds nothing relevant.
- Value prop is aspirational ("Build a Bigger Future") but light on *risk-reversal* ("Refused before? We specialise in re-applications," "Free profile evaluation in 24h").

**Missing trust signals** — Google rating block, review count, counsellor bios, university partner logos, certification badges, embedded Google Map, "as featured in," visa-grant screenshots (redacted), success-story metrics with dates.

**Missing urgency** — No intake-deadline countdowns ("Jan 2026 intake — applications close in X days"), no seat/slot scarcity for free counselling, no seasonal banners tied to filing deadlines.

**Missing social proof** — Live Google reviews, video testimonials with names+countries+year, "X students placed this month," WhatsApp-style success snippets.

**Missing FAQs** — Good on home, but **none on countries, services, eligibility, or (non-existent) visitor/PR pages** — exactly where buyers have objections.

**Form friction** (measured from the code)
- *Counsellor form:* 5 required fields (First name, Last name, Email, Phone, **Mode of counselling required**) + optional destination. **Friction points:** (1) First+Last split (use one "Full name"); (2) **Email required** — in India, phone/WhatsApp converts far better and many drop at email; (3) **"Preferred mode" required** before submit adds a decision. *Recommended minimum:* Name + WhatsApp + Country. Make email and mode optional.
- *Eligibility form:* 5 steps, contact captured at step 5 — well-designed and the multi-step commitment is good. Risk: drop-off before step 5 means *no lead captured at all* (contact is last). Consider capturing phone at step 2–3 or offering "WhatsApp me my matches."

**Conversion blockers (summary list)**
1. No WhatsApp button. 2. No sticky mobile CTA. 3. No Meta Pixel/conversion events (can't optimise paid). 4. Email required on primary form. 5. No risk-reversal/urgency. 6. Unproven trust claims. 7. No pages for half the services the business sells. 8. No exit-intent (only a 45s soft modal, once per visitor).

**Home page — what's working (keep):** premium hero, clear dual CTA (Book / Check Eligibility), strong stat block, university-matcher, AEO FAQ component, success carousel fed from CRM, GA4 installed, solid technical SEO base (canonical, OG, JSON-LD EducationalOrganization with both branches).

## Part 2 – Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO)

Each recommendation: **Why it matters · Expected impact · Priority · Difficulty · Estimated lift.** Lift estimates are directional (Tier-2 visa-lead benchmarks; validate via your own A/B once Pixel/GA4 events exist). Priorities: **P0** = do this week, **P1** = this month, **P2** = later.

### A. Floating WhatsApp + Sticky Mobile CTA Bar
- **Why:** WhatsApp is the dominant lead/comms channel in India; mobile is ~75–85% of your traffic. Right now mobile users must open a menu to call and *cannot* WhatsApp at all.
- **Build:** Persistent bottom bar on mobile (Call · WhatsApp · Book) + a floating WA bubble on desktop. Pre-fill: *"Hi TESCA, I'm interested in [page topic]. Please share details."* Fire GA4 `generate_lead` + Meta `Lead` on click.
- **Impact:** High · **P0** · Easy (½ day) · **+15–30% total lead volume.**

### B. Meta Pixel + GA4 conversion events (measurement foundation)
- **Why:** Without it you can't run optimised lead campaigns, build lookalikes, or retarget. This is a *dependency*, not an option.
- **Build:** Add Pixel in `Layout.astro`; fire `Lead` on counsellor submit, eligibility step-5 submit, inquiry submit, WA click, Call click. Mirror as GA4 key events. Add server-side CAPI later (you already have a Cloudflare/Supabase backend that can relay).
- **Impact:** Foundational · **P0** · Easy–Medium (1 day) · enables everything in Part 6.

### C. Hero headline + sub-headline (intent-matched variants)
- **Why:** "Don't Just Study Abroad. Build a Bigger Future." is brand-led, not intent-led. Paid traffic converts on *specificity + risk-reversal*.
- **Test (study LP):** *"Get Your 2026 Study Visa — Free Profile Evaluation in 24 Hours."* Sub: *"20 years • 30,000+ students • 97% visa success. Tell us your scores, get a university shortlist + visa plan on WhatsApp."*
- **Impact:** High · **P1** · Easy · **+8–15% on LP CVR.**

### D. Reduce primary-form friction
- **Why:** Email-required + First/Last + required "mode" = 3 unnecessary drop points.
- **Build:** Single "Full name," **WhatsApp number** as the key field, Country (optional), email optional, mode optional/removed. Add "or WhatsApp us" link beneath submit.
- **Impact:** High · **P1** · Easy · **+10–20% form completion.**

### E. Proof-led trust block (replace claims with evidence)
- **Why:** Self-claims ("97%") underperform verifiable proof.
- **Build:** Google rating + review count (live), 3–4 *real* video testimonials (name, country, intake year), redacted visa-grant snapshots, university-partner logos, counsellor bios with photos + credentials (IELTS-trainer cert, years), ICEF/British Council badges *if held*.
- **Impact:** High · **P1** · Medium · **+10–25% on CVR for cold traffic.**

### F. Urgency & seasonality
- **Why:** July = Jan-intake recruitment + Sept-intake filing deadlines. Deadlines drive action.
- **Build:** Intake-deadline countdown banner ("January 2026 intake — priority applications close soon"), "limited free counselling slots this week," seat-style scarcity on the booking modal.
- **Impact:** Medium–High · **P1** · Easy · **+5–12%.**

### G. Booking flow → calendar + instant WhatsApp confirmation
- **Why:** Today the form emails the team; the lead waits. Speed-to-lead is the #1 predictor of conversion.
- **Build:** After submit, show a Calendly-style slot picker *and* auto-send a WhatsApp confirmation. Even an interim "We'll WhatsApp you within 10 min (Mon–Sat 10–7)" sets expectation.
- **Impact:** High · **P1** · Medium · **+10–20% lead→consultation.**

### H. Eligibility checker — capture earlier + WhatsApp delivery
- **Why:** Contact is captured only at step 5; pre-step-5 drop-offs are lost entirely.
- **Build:** Add "Get my matches on WhatsApp" at step 5 (deep value), and a soft micro-capture (just phone) after step 2 with "save my progress." Keep the excellent results dashboard.
- **Impact:** Medium–High · **P1** · Medium · **+8–15% captured leads.**

### I. Exit-intent + scroll-depth CTAs
- **Why:** Your only popup is a 45s timer, once per visitor. No exit-intent, no scroll triggers.
- **Build:** Desktop exit-intent → lead-magnet offer ("Free 2026 Country Eligibility Guide — get it on WhatsApp"); mobile 60% scroll-depth → sticky "Check eligibility free" nudge.
- **Impact:** Medium · **P2** · Easy · **+3–8%.**

### J. Lead magnets gated by WhatsApp (not just email)
- **Why:** Checklists/guides are proven micro-conversions; deliver via WhatsApp to start the conversation.
- **Build:** "Canada PR 2026 Checklist," "USA Visitor Visa Document List," "Germany Opportunity Card Guide" — deliver instantly on WhatsApp, tag the lead by interest.
- **Impact:** Medium–High · **P1** · Medium · **+10–20% new top-of-funnel leads.**

### K. FAQ schema everywhere (objection handling = CRO + AEO)
- **Why:** FAQs handle objections at the point of doubt and win featured snippets/AI answers.
- **Build:** Add FAQ + `FAQPage` schema to countries, services, eligibility, and each new visitor/PR page.
- **Impact:** Medium · **P1** · Easy · **+3–8% CVR, + organic visibility.**

### L. Footer / nav fixes
- Fix broken LinkedIn link; remove or relocate dev-portfolio + Security-Audit links from prime footer; add **Contact, About, WhatsApp, and Visas** menu items.
- **Impact:** Low–Medium · **P2** · Easy.

SEO is a *compounding* channel — it won't produce most of July's 100 leads, but the work done in July seeds Aug–Oct. Pair it with paid for July volume.

## Part 11 – Lead Funnel

### Full funnel with drop-off diagnosis & automation

```
TRAFFIC (Meta/Google/Organic/GBP/Referral)
   │  ▼ biggest leak: weak/slow LP, no WhatsApp option
LANDING PAGE / WHATSAPP / INSTANT FORM
   │  ▼ leak: long form, email required, no proof
LEAD CAPTURED (Name + WhatsApp + interest)  ← optimise for THIS event
   │  ▼ BIGGEST LEAK: slow follow-up (speed-to-lead)
INSTANT WHATSAPP + 10-MIN CALLBACK
   │  ▼ leak: no nurture, single touch
COUNSELLOR APPOINTMENT BOOKED
   │  ▼ leak: no reminders → no-shows (30–50%)
CONSULTATION HELD (qualified)
   │  ▼ leak: no proposal follow-up
DOCUMENT COLLECTION
   │
APPLICATION FILED → VISA APPROVED → REVIEW + REFERRAL (recycle to top)
```

### Where leads die (and the fix)

| Stage | Typical drop | Root cause | Automation fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| Traffic→LP | 60–80% bounce | slow page / wrong intent / no WhatsApp | sticky WA, intent-matched LP, fast load |
| LP→Lead | 95–98% don't convert | form friction, no trust, email required | WhatsApp-first, proof, ≤3 fields |
| Lead→Contact | **30–50% never reached** | slow/inconsistent follow-up | **instant auto-WhatsApp + 10-min SLA + tele-call** |
| Contact→Booked | 40–60% | no incentive/urgency | slot picker + "free, limited slots" |
| Booked→Held | 30–50% no-show | no reminders | T-24h + T-2h WhatsApp reminders |
| Held→Client | 60–80% don't close immediately | no nurture | 10-email + WhatsApp drip + offer |

### Automation opportunities (mapped to your stack)
- You already have a **Supabase CRM + `/admin/leads`**. Wire new leads to: (1) instant WhatsApp template, (2) Slack/WhatsApp alert to the on-duty counsellor, (3) tag by source/interest, (4) enrol in nurture.
- **Speed-to-lead is the highest-ROI automation.** Leads contacted within 5 minutes convert dramatically better than 30+ minutes. Automate the *first* touch so no lead waits.
- **Lead scoring:** auto-prioritise by interest (PR/study = high value), recency, and "refused before = hot."
